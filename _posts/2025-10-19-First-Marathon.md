![schedule]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/images/2025-10-19/schedule.png)

Something I found really helpful was AI. For both exams I uploaded lecture material (scripts, slides, background books, old exams) to NotebookLM and asked it to generate practice questions based on that material. I then copied it and asked Copilot (because it has a better UI and is better for conversationsal general questions that are not directly based on any source) to nicely format it and then I went through the practice questions asking copilot to correct my answers and answer questions I had. At the end I let it generate a report of how I did. What areas did I do well in and which one would need work. I then used this summary to generate a new set of questions from NotebookLM. For the time series exercises I went as far as making these summaries and the old exams part of its sources so it had more context. I think without it, I would have never been able to get to this level this quickly.

### Race Recap

The day has come‚Ä¶ After hundreds of kilometers of training, it is was time to run my first marathon. It is something I wrote on my bucket list a few years, not thinking much about it. ![marathon training]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/images/2025-10-19/marathon-training.png)  

I did think about my training throughout the summer and tried to do it in a structured way. In June I tried to work on speed which concluded in a 5km time trial which I finished in 20:43. Then in July I cycled a lot during Marie and my bike trip to France and back. I think it did give me some fatigue resistance because of the amount of time spent moving, but I am not sure how much aerobic stimulus I got from it. In August then my goal was to get back into running and build towards starting an 8 week marathon trainingsplan made by a running coaching couple whos podcast I listen to a lot. I think these final 8 weeks gave me a major boost. Once I arrived in Toronto I started running a lot. The plan was really well structured with general mileage progression, workout variety, and tempo parts in long runs. During my longest training run three weeks out I had some GI issues towards the end and belly pain strong enough to keep me from running well. Two and a half weeks out then I ran, like the plan suggested, a 10km time trial to see where my fitness is at. I ran it in 42:19, which is a lot faster than I expected. Extrapolating from there gave me confidence that I could run even faster than I ever thought possible. My main goal until the end was to stay under 4 hours being really sure I would be able to do it. Realistically, I thought, sub 3:45 could be possible, 3:40 if I had a really good day. During the final two weeks then I ran marathon efforts in training faster to see how my heart rate responds. The weekend after the 10km though I felt sever ankle pain at the end of a long run which forced me to do cross training for a week to let it recover. Luckily for me I was able to run pain free again just a week later.

**Fueling:** 

Pre-Race:

- At home: some cereal + Water
- ca 30 minutes before start: Cliff Bar (43g Carbs)

During Race:

- Strategy went perfectly: A GU Gel (23g Carbs some with Caffein) every 4km (about every 20 minutes)
- Total: 10 x 23g = 230g = 65g Carbs/hour

Hydration:

- I had my own bottle which was absolutely clutch.
- I refilled it about 4 or 5 times at aid stations.
- They had nuun which added electrolytes which was great. Either pure that or mixed with water to make it less sweet.
- On the last few km I got really thirsty so I drank two cups of water but didn‚Äôt really get anything out of it because it is impossible to drink out of a cup while running. (also the amount of cups on the floor around these aid stations‚Ä¶)
